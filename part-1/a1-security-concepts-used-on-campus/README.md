# A1 - Security Concepts Used on Campus

## Overview
This activity examines security concepts used on campus. The aim is to identify practical security measures, explain how they are applied, and reflect on how they help protect people, property, systems, and information.

## Campus area investigated
- University of Western Australia 

## Security concepts identified
- Access control
- Authentication
- Surveillance and monitoring
- Network security
- Data protection

## Description and purpose

### 1. Access control
- **Security concept:**
  Access control
- **Description:**  
  Access control is used on campus to restrict entry to certain buildings, rooms, or areas. I observed this through the [card reader] located at the entryways to certain rooms and buildings on the campus. This shows that not all parts of the campus are open to everyone.
- **Purpose:**  
The purpose of access control is to ensure that only authorised people can enter sensitive or restricted spaces.
- **How it improves security:**  
  This improves security by reducing the risk of unauthorised entry, theft, disruption, or access to sensitive university areas and resources.

### 2. Authentication
- **Security concept:** Authentication
- **Description:**
  Authentication is used on campus to verify a user’s identity before they can access university systems or services. I observed this through [LMS login, student connect login and email login]. This shows that users must prove who they are before access is granted.
- **Purpose:**  
  The purpose of authentication is to confirm that a person is the legitimate user of an account or service.
- **How it improves security:**  
  This improves security by preventing unauthorised access to systems and making activity more accountable, since usage is linked to an identified user.
- **Evidence:**  
  ![evidence/student-login.png](https://github.com/Justin2204/Portfolio/blob/main/Screenshot%202026-03-09%20224923.png?raw=true)

### 3. Surveillance and monitoring
- **Security concept:** Surveillance and monitoring
- **Description:**  
  Surveillance and monitoring are used on campus to observe activity and support safety. I observed this through [CCTV cameras, security signage]. This shows that the campus uses visible monitoring measures to watch for incidents or inappropriate behaviour.
- **Purpose:**  
  The purpose of surveillance and monitoring is to deter misconduct, detect incidents, and support incident response.
- **How it improves security:**  
  This improves security by discouraging theft, vandalism, or unsafe behaviour, while also providing evidence if an incident occurs.

### 4. Network security
- **Security concept:** Network security
- **Description:**  
  Network security is used on campus to manage and protect digital access to university systems and internet services. I observed this through [secure Wi-Fi login, separate guest/student networks, sign-in before access]. This shows that network access is controlled rather than openly available without restrictions.
- **Purpose:**  
  The purpose of network security is to control who can access digital services and reduce misuse of university networks.
- **How it improves security:**  
  This improves security by limiting unauthorised access, helping manage who uses the network, and supporting safer access to online university resources.

### 5. Data protection
- **Security concept:** Data protection
- **Description:**  
  Data protection is used on campus to protect sensitive information such as student records, academic details, and personal information. I observed this through [student portal login, privacy policy]. This shows that sensitive information is not openly accessible and is protected through controlled access.
- **Purpose:**  
  The purpose of data protection is to keep personal and academic information secure and only available to authorised users.
- **How it improves security:**  
  This improves security by reducing the risk of privacy breaches, unauthorised disclosure, or misuse of sensitive information.
